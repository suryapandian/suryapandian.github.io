What a Time to Be Alive

Published on March 22, 2026

As a software engineer, I'm getting very used to the day-to-day productivity gains and occasional time sinks of AI. But lately, I've been struck by something deeper: the sheer contextual breadth of these models.

We are talking about a knowledge base that spans from the advent of language to the latest immigration codes.

Two Examples

  1. I prompted a 1,500-year-old Sanskrit hymn's title, and it built a word-for-word translation of the poem: guru-ashtakam.html
  2. I prompted "Immigration policy in country X," and it built a compliance checker from scratch: uk-ilr-citizenship.netlify.app

The kicker? I didn't feed it the policy or the verses. It already "knew".

Cost: < $4
Time invested: 2–3 hours
Without AI: 1+ year (or 1 week fulltime)
Disclaimer: Yes, AI hallucinates. We still need to be the "human in the loop" to double-check every line. But even with that caveat, I can't help but think... what a time to be alive. Woah!
